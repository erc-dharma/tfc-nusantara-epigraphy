Hands:

The hand is the one typically seen in Singasari-period inscriptions.

The inscription hardly seems to know the long vowels ū and ī, most expected spellings ū and ī figuring as u and i.

Apparatus

1r1 van • The last akṣara of the preceding plate must have been la, to make lavan·.
1r3-1r4 asiḍaktan·|{l asiḍaktan·} • It seems the scribe hesitated around the transition from line 3 to 4, needlessly repeating the sequence lasiḍaktan· after the line-filler sign at the end of l. 3
1v5 Avə: • Correct Avī?
2v1 tutu¡ṇ!⟨ñ⟩juṁ • Cf. tutuñjuṅin Sarvadharma 5v4 and Kakurugan 4v5–6 (MNI E. 22). — 2v1 i pə: ṁ • Cf. OJED s.vv. ipəṅ, ipuṅ, pə̄ṅ and puṅ (Zoetmulder 1982: 695–696, 697, 1342, 1445). Under pə̄ṅ, an instance from Waringin Pitu 13r6 is interpreted as kǝmbaṅ iṅ pə̄ṅ. This can be supported by Kancana 5v2, where we find kəmbaṅ iṁ puṁ. See also Kambang Putih back, line 18: kambaṅ i puṅ. From the Singasari period, the Sarvadharma inscription (5v4 kǝmbaṅ i pǝ: ṁ) was used in OJED as the sole occurrence justifying its entry ipəṅ. The Majapahit-period occurrences clearly suggest that kǝmbaṅ i pǝṅ and not kǝmbaṅ ipǝṅ was intended.
2v4 pasək-pa| • The first akṣaras of the following plate must have contained sək, to make pasək-pasǝk.

Translation by Arlo Griffiths

1r1-1r3 ... with the Kalagyan; turns eastward heading for Bugǝl; turns southwestward at Bugǝl, adjoining the boundary with with Bugǝl; turns southeastward at Bugǝl, adjoining the boundary with Bugǝl, sharply turns northeastward adjoining the boundary with Paḍaṅ, cutting through the river.

1r3-1r5The Nine Lands cut through the river northeastward; eastward they cut the river at Pagajutan, southward adjoining the military camp kabalān, turn southward adjoining the boundary with Lipuṅ, eastward at the Guṅguṅ river, adjoining the boundary with Limbeyyan, turn southward adjoining Lipuṅ.

1r5-1v1Moreover the holy foundation has possessions to be taken by lance from thieves ḍampǝlan, i.e. ḍampulan.

1v1Moreover the holy foundation of the freehold at the Nine has dependencies: the villages Hǝmbǝk, yielding 5 guilders of tahil, [and] Amuṅgul.

1v1-2r2Moreover the freehold at the Nine, as grant of the prince of Jaṅgala, has been given the privilege of not being under authority of the servants vinava of the king. None of all the former ones may meddle with it: Vulus and minor Vulus, such as Miśras and minor Miśras, the Paṅuraṅ, the Pakudur, the Vadihati, the Aniga, the Pamaṇikan, the Manimpiki, the Malandaṅ, the Lǝca, the Limus Galuh, the Ramanaṅ, the Viniṅle, the Kataṅgaran, the Sukun, the Halu Varak ‘Rhinoceros Club’, the Racǝmǝṅ, the overseer of harlots, the overseer of gambling, the overseer of Huñjǝman, the overseer of cock-fights ?, tǝgǝl, the overseer of [cock-]cages, the Ǝlǝblǝb, the Kutak, the Kukap, the Sinagiha, the Taṅkil, the Salyut, the Avir, the Avǝ̄, the Tǝpuṅ Kavuṅ, the Suṅsuṅ Paṅhuraṅ, the Sikǝpan, the Tirvan, the Maṅhuri, the Taṅhiran, the Lūdan, the Tūtan, the Alaṅan , the horse-groom, the mahout, the quail-herd, the Padavut Paṅpaṅ, the Pakikis, the Pavǝlit, the Donan , the Vuran , all requisites of all the royal servants. They do not set foot in the holy freehold at the Nine.

2r2-2r4Moreover the men inside i.e., the courtiers: the dwarves, the cripples, the albinos, the liliputs, the Pujut, the Kǝmir, the Boṇḍan: they do not set foot in the holy freehold at the Nine.

2r4-2v3Moreover shall be given as privilege to all brethren in the Nine: the raising of children, to be allowed to brandish a white parasol, to play the curiṅ accompanied by kaṅsi; to (have bells) tinkle by day and by night; to have ivory bamboo as bench; to use gold-boreh; to make offerings of (the type) vataṅ, siddhayuga, aṅgī, buntǝr, śānti, kukuvaka ; to wear cloth: nāgapuṣpa or navagraha, ivory bench, tuvuh vatu, hurip anak, flower on the Pǝṅ, tutuñjuṅ, stem of varyaṅ — all kinds of dress; to be allowed to play gǝṇḍiṅ in childish manner ; to lead pigs; to fasten a loincloth with a cord; to lean on a bow; to shoulder a trough while wearing banantǝn; to have the (children’s) hair cut above a pavilion;See Boechari 2012: 42. An alternative interpretation is ‘to keep scissors’ — see translation of Sarvadharma. throughout the raising of children, they have no attachment.

2v3-2v4Thus was the wish-granting of the prince of Jaṅgala to all brethren in the Nine, an independent adǝg riṅgit freehold.
