Commentary

An alternative designation for this inscription is Kembang Arum.

Distinctive features of the script include:

  • The comma punctuation takes shape of median circle.
  • The tarung is attached in the manner of p and h even to other consonants (as in no, so, etc.)
  • The anusvāra sometimes used for phonemic/m/ in the word inuṁ “drink” (3r20, 3v3), but sometimes we find the usual spelling inum· (3v4).
  • Pasangan ḍ is sometimes not clearly distinguished from pasangan d, resulting in non-standard pairings with ṇ: e.g. Aṇdabhuvaṇa, paṇdavutan

After the end of the inscription on 3v13, there are a number of faint marks that resemble writing, but are not clearly legible.

On the ceremonies involving the sīmastones, see Titi Surti Nastiti 2018: 189–191.
