Manah i Manuk

Metadata

Current Version:  draft, 2023-04-04Z

Editor:   Marine Schoettel.

DHARMA Identifier: INSIDENKManahimanuk

Hand Description: This inscription uses an unusual shape of basic punctuation, which does not look like a comma but rather like a simple vertical bar or daṇḍa. The pasangan s, ṣ and p are written in the "modern" fashion on the same line, instead of below the line as it is still usually the case in the Majapahit period. We observe two shapes of anusvāra in this inscription, with the alternative round shape appearing for instance l. 2v5. The scribe uses two ways of forming akṣara lā: with tarung placed to the right of the akṣara, most frequent with tarung written in “superscript” form: 3r1 prajālāṅkarā

Translation by Marine Schoettel

1v1-1v5by royal consecration name, Dyah Hayam Vuruk, being accompanied by the order of Her Majesty the illustrious Tribhuvanottuṅgarājadevī Jayaviṣṇuvardhanī, an image of the Goddess who is auspicious for all kings (i.e. Śrī-Lakṣmī?), splendid by the glory of her charming and captivating knowledges, [in union] like an Ardhanārīśvara along with His Majesty the illustrious Lord Kr̥tavardhana, adorned by the obeisance of all kings, together with the order of Her Majesty the illustrious Vijayadevī, a perfect ornament of heroism mounted in the royal diadem of a Great King, possessed of a mind sprouting with all virtues, [in union] like an Ardhanārīśvara together with His Majesty the illustrious Lord Vijayarājasa, adorned by his heroism on the battlefield.

2r1-2r2[In each case their order] were received by the Three rakryan Great Ministers, i.e. the rakryan great minister of Hino, Dyah Īśvara, the rakryan great minister of Sirikan, Dyah Ipo, the rakryan great minister of Halu, Dyah Kañciṅ,

2r2-2v4It came down to all the ranking officials in the council : the Honorable army general Pu Tanu; the Honorable ātmarāja Pu Taṇḍiṅ ; the rakryan Dmuṅ Pu Gaṣṭi, the rakryan Kanuruhan Pu Turut; the rakryan Raṅga Pu Lurukan; the rakryan Tuməṅguṅ Pu Nāla, protector of virtuous people and dedicated to subduing wicked people, bearing the title of chief minister (patih) of Pajaṅ, accompanied by the rake head of the royal guard (paṅalasan) Pu Pṭul, possessed of good policy and discipline; foremost of them the minister who is an ornament of the [king’s] subjects skilled in interpreting people’s behavior , the rake chief minister Pu Mada, a commander who is like Vr̥haspati in all matters of policies , a preserver of the life of the illustrious Great King: a pedestal bolstering the reign of the illustrious Great King who is like a reflection of Īśvara, causing good actions to be carried out in the sphere of the island of Java, as well as [causing] the destruction of those who attack His Majesty the illustrious Great King;

2v4-3v2the administrator of Śaiva affairs, the Honorable rājaparākrama, the revered master [called] Dharmarāja, the administrator of Buddhist domains/affairs, the Honorable adhirāja, the revered master [called] Kanakamuni, consummate in Buddhist doctrine and Grammar, and moreover , the speakers/teachers of religious law (dharma-pravaktā), who settle litigations (vyavahāra-vicchedaka):

  • the officiant of Tirvan, the Honorable vaṅśādhirāja, the revered master [called] Śivanātha, of the Bhairava order, consummate in the sciences of Nyāya and Grammar;
  • the officiant of Kaṇḍamuhi, the revered master [called] Marmmanātha, with the byname Āṅśuman, of the Saura order, consummate in the sciences of Nyāya and Grammar;
  • the officiant of Maṅhuri, the revered master [called] Smaranātha, of the Bhairava order, consummate in the sciences of Nyāya and Grammar;
  • the officiant of Jamba, the revered master [called] Jayasmara, of the Saura order, consummate in the science of Sāṁkhya;
  • the officiant of Pamvatan, the revered master [called] Agreśvara, of the Bhairava order, consummate in the sciences of Nyāya and Grammar;
  • the officiant of Kaṇḍaṅan junior, the revered master [called] Munīndra, consummate in Buddhist science/doctrine,
,

3v2-3v5Having contemplated the reasons for the strength of the reliability (adhimukti) of both litigants , this is how their litigation was settled by the judges who are steadfast in the keeping of the (dharma) for lawsuits inside the capital. That is the reason why, , together, both litigants appeared respectfully one after another (?, mampak-āmpak) to report to all the ranking officials in the advisory council, presenting their respective cases.

4r1As for the testimony (suṅan vara-varah) of Aki Santana, with the byname Śaraṇa, along with all his companions, ki Karṇa, with the byname Manākara, Ajaran Reka, ki Siran, ki Jumput:

4r1-4v1“We own this land [called] Manah i Manuk. The distribution (kahayvan) of the irrigated rice fields: in Aḍavuṅ 1 lirih; in Berəm, 1 lirih; in Pajənon south of the habitations, 2 lirih; in Pajənon a garden [of] 1 kuñci; in Paṅeran a garden [of] 1 kuñci; in Tiga Thani the distribution of the irrigated rice fields [is]: in Vareṅ 1 lirih, the distribution in Valuntas, 10 lirih; in Tiga Thani a garden [of] 1 kuñci. 16 lirih were given away by my ancestors to the “place of recluses” of Pakaṇḍaṅan; to the maṇḍala-order in Kukub, 2 lirih [were given]; 1 lirih [was given] to the jaṅgan (‘rural doctors, see also SKK 11.24 hayaṅ ñaho di (568)pupuja di saṅgar ma, patah, puja daun, gәlar (569)palajaṅ, puja kәmbaṅ, (570)ñampiṅan liṅga, (571)ṅomean saṅ hyaṅ, siṅ savatәk muja ma, (572)jaṅgan taña.’ for Pigeaud ) in Paṅle. [and besides that, land] which was enjoyed by those who pay up taxes: 33 lirih, in total 67 lirih. This has been in continuous possession/enjoyment (sthiti-bukti) from my ancestors: my father, my grandfather, great-grandfather, great-great-grandfather, great-great-great-great-grandfather, great-great-great-great-great-grandfather. There is no concrete evidence (suvanda), for this enjoyment has existed from time immemorial. It has for clear evidence the existence of the pious foundation of my ancestors, namely the pavilion at the maṇḍala of Kasəḍahan, dated 919 Śaka, which name is said to be the “Vayu tree in the middle” ((vayu anəṅah)). That dates from the same time as the irrigated rice-field. And moreover, these are no irrigated ricefields of the relatives established at Sīma Tiga beyond the boundary of Pakaṇḍaṅan. It does not yield tahil, it is not liable to be designated, it should not be used as a tool by the region (deśa).
