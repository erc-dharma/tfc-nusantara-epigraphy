INSIDENKmanahimanuk

Hands:

This inscription uses an unusual shape of basic punctuation, which does not look like a comma but rather like a simple vertical bar or daṇḍa. The pasangan <s>, <ṣ> and <p> are written in the modern fashion (?) on the same line, and not below the line as it is still usually the case in the Majapahit period. We observe two shapes of anusvāra in this inscription, with the alternative round shape appearing for instance l. 2v5. The scribe uses two ways of forming akṣara lā: with tarung placed to the right of the akṣara, most frequent with tarung written in “superscript” form: 3r1 prajālāṅkarā
