Translation by van Naerssen 1941

1v1-2Hail! Elapsed Śaka year 824, month of Śravaṇa, fifteenth (tithi) of the waxing fortnight, Paniron, Paiṅ‚ Tuesday, [in the vuku] Maḍaṅ Kuṅan, seventh karaṇa, position of the moon: pūrvasādha, declination: Śiva.

1v2-4That was the time when the Great King, Lord (rake) of Vatu Kura, Prince Balituṅ, the Illustrious Īśvarakeśavotsavatuṅga, bestowed upon the village elders of Vatu Kura a demarcation fee of 1 māṣa kā for the place of his (i.e. Balituṅ’s) ancestral foundation (dharma paṅasthūlan). It is to be worshipped by the village elders of Vatukura every full moon of the month of Bhādrapada,

1v4-2r4In addition, the irrigated rice fields, the dry rice fields, the wet rice fields, and the gardens, they are to contribute the sum of 1 māṣa in silver per head. The community (duvan) of Babadan is to contribute 3 māṣa. [The village of] Mata Panas is to contribute 1 māṣa of gold, 4 māṣa, Payaman 1 māṣa of gold, 4 māṣa, Buhara Suvul 5 māṣa, Buhara Uṇḍuh 3 māṣa, Buhara Təṅah 5 māṣa, Buhara Hoya 5 māṣa, Buhara Paṅanten 2 māṣa, Bareṅ 10 māṣa, also the floral-tribute (lit. making of flowerbaskets) [is to be paid] 2 māṣa ku and unpolished rice to the value of 1 ku per household (lit. ‘per door’) and moreover 1 māṣa ku and unpolished rice to the value of 1 ku per household for the Panaman festival on the day of the full moon in the month of Jyaiṣṭha. Further, 1 māṣa ku and unpolished rice to the value of 1 ku per household on the day of the full moon the month of Caitra.

2r4-Further, the ravi galuh (the jewel-radiant sun?) of Babadan is to give wooden buckets, salvers, the akṣenya of the god of the foundation, besides his head ornament. These constitute the offerings of the village elders to the god of the foundation. The remains (of the offerings) shall belong to the persons who take part in the work: those who officiate at the offerings, at the upakalpa sacred functions, those who sweep (the temple ground), the servants of the temple, the pasinghir, all the people of merit who guard the god. They shall have [the remains]. Moreover, the remains are to be used for the festive garments to be made by the village elders, viz. rings for the upper arm and the forearm and ornaments provided with the Śrī symbol in honour of the god of the foundation. [...]...

Commentary

1v3panīma: see other instances of payment of a demarcation fee?

1v4dharmma paṅasthūlan: is this Balitung’s deification temple?

Bibliography

First edited by J.L.A. Brandes 1913, apparently based on autopsy of the plate; readings of small segments of text containing proper names and toponyms by L.-Ch. Damais (1970) based on a mediocre eye-copy that he had been able to consult at the Archaeological Service; reproduction of Brandes’ edition, to which is added a translation into English, by H. B. Sarkar (1971-1972). The text is re-edited by Arlo Griffiths without inspection of any reproduction, taking Brandes’ edition as base-text, with reinterpretation of the transliteration system according to DHARMA norms, and adoption of new readings proposed by Damais.
