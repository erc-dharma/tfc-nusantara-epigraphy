    Apparatus

    7 săkṣăt· ◇ sakr̥t· B • The reading is ambiguous and in the absence of other cases of pasangan ṣ or vowel marker r̥ in this inscription, it is hard to exclude Brandes’ reading. My choice is based mainly on the fact that the word sākṣāt is typical of this kind of context whereas the Sanskrit word sakr̥t, which Brandes thought he recognized, does not seem to have entered Old Javanese lexicon at all.

    Translation by Arlo Griffiths

    for the Śrī Paduka Mpuṅku is at once (sakr̥t) the Lords Brahma, (8) Viṣṇu and Maheśvara. They punish any kind of disturbance of the grant of the Śrī Paduka (9) Mpuṅku. The Lords Tripuruṣa punish it, accompanied by all the five (10) great holy bhūtas, especially the devatas. That is the reason that (11) the threefold king (haji tiga-tiga) will have the power.

    Commentary

    Of this inscription engraved on a large stela, text remains on only one principal face, more particularly the end of the inscription which must have begun on the opposite principal face, it being unknown whether the lateral faces were ever engraved. The reconstructions at the beginnings and ends of several lines proposed here are in several cases somewhat tentative.

    Bibliography

    First edited by Brandes (1913); re-edited here by Arlo Griffiths based on an orthophoto extracted from a 3D-model. Brandes’ edition, based on an estampage, left open gaps at the beginnings and ends of most lines.
