The Pamintihan charter

Metadata

Current Version:  draft, 2023-04-05Z

Editor:   Arlo Griffiths.

DHARMA Identifier: INSIDENKPamintihan

Hand Description: According to De Casparis (1975), the charter ‘is written in traditional Majapahit script’. The scholar rightly points out the curiosity of the akṣara ma, ‘written in two parts, not unlike the ma of the Ādityavarman inscriptions.’ Relying only on a photo of the first plate, he was of course unable to observe the subtle but unmistakable change in the script on the fifth plate, where ma is written in normal manner. • k and t are either sometimes confused or written in such a manner that k looks like t (kuvera, aṅlakvakən)

Commentary

Discovered as a set of 4 out of an original set of 5 plates. Plate number 2 was not among the discovered plates. Plate number 4 has been lost after its first decipherment by Bosch. Only plates 1, 3 and 5 presently remain accessible. Decoration with plant on stand and bird in left margin of 1v.

Bibliography

First deciphered by Bosch with a translation into Dutch of a small section; this edition reproduced but furnish with a cimplete translation into Indonesian by Muhammad Yamin; the text of plates 1, 3 and 5 re-edited by Boechari.
