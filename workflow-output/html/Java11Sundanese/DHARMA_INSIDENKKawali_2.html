Apparatus

1 Aya ma • On the use of particle ma in Old Sundanese, see Aditia Gunawan and Fuji Evi Fauziah (Forthcoming)
6 najəṙ • read. nañjər. Cf. SKK.1 that relate the word tañjər with praṅ (synonim of juritan): dəgdəg tañjər jaya praṅ ‘firm, superior, victorious in the battle’.
7 na • cf. ḍina in Kawali I. See Dict. under na, abbreviation of dina.

Translation

If one occupies this (place called) Kawali, let him adopt the proper behavior, so that he remains upright in battle.

Commentary

Lateral faces (1 = top, 2 = right, 3 = bottom, 4 = left). First read by Pleyte (1911: 196). Our text follows that of Noorduyn (1988: 309–310), which we have confirmed by consulting our own photographs. Noorduyn has observed that this text is in verse form.
