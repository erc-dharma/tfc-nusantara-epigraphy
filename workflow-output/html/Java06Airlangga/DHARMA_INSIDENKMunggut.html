Apparatus

1.5 kumonakən ikanaṁ ◇ kumonakən nikanaṁDamais 1955
1.6 babadnya, maṅaran· ◇ papadya maṅaranMachiSuhadi & RichadianaKartakusuma 1996 NinnySusantiTejowasono 2003 — 1.6 kilanogajusa • The apparent anusvāra on akṣara la seems to be due to an irregularity in the stone, so we ignore it.
1.18 pa(gə)pagəḥ • For a similar phrase, see Kusambyan, face A and Sima Angalayang 13v3 saṁ hyaṁ Ājñā haji pagəḥpagəḥ.
2.5 saṁ maṅilala vuluvulu ri daṅū • It seems some words have been omitted here, because we expect saṁ maṅilala drabya haji vuluvulu ri daṅū.
2.7 ramanaṅ s(va)ra gəṇḍiṁ • The reading is rather uncertain, all the more so as the words svara gəṇḍiṁ are not found in any of the numerous other instances of this kind of list known to us.
2.11 paba(yai) • We would like to be able to read pabaye (as in Cane and Turun Hyang in the same contexts), but that seems difficult, so we opt for yai as close equivalent to account for the stroke seen above the akṣara. Another possibility would be to interpret that stroke as a layar and read pabarye (see another possible layar of this kind if one of the alternative readings with layar is to be adopted instead of Amutimuti at the end of line 22).
2.17 An(n) ikanaṁ • The pasangan looks more like t than n, but we need pasangan n here. Cf. face d, line 17.
2.18 ¿ra (s)aṁ hyaṅ?⟨rasa saṁ hyaṁ⟩ • Cf. Sima Anglayang 13v6.
2.20 A(mutimuti) • Various alternatives readings are imaginable, depending on whether the word ended at the end of line 22, or whether anything was engraved before rvaṁ at the beginning of line 23, and on how the horizontal stroke above the penultimate akṣaras of line 22 is explained. Neither the reading tentatively adopted here, nor any of the alternatives we have considered (Amutərmutəra, Amutimutiḥ, Amutirmutəḥ, Amutimutiḥ), yield a word that is expected in this context.
3.4-3.5 tamolah ata(ḥ paṅa)sthāna • The reading is rather uncertain, and the word paṅasthāna not recorded in OJED (which only records paṅasthāna), although we find it in a similar context in Sima Anglayang 4v4–5: yāvat tamolaḥ I saṁ hyaṁ sarvvadharmma, mvaṁ paṅasthāna ri jātakanira.
3.7 (A)nn ikanaṁ • Instead of A, it would be easier to read sa, but this is hard to accept in the context. The turn of phrase ann ikanaṅ was also found above, in C, line 19.
3.18 ¿keñcaka?⟨kencaka⟩ • No punctuation sign seems to have been engraved between this word and the next.
3.27-3.28 tumūtak(na) yacānyayan ika masthānabati ri • we are unable to understand this sequence, and therefore uncertain about some of the readings as well as the word divisions. We suspect yacānyayan may be a scribal error for yathānyayan or yavānya yan, but in neither case do we obtain an understandable text. More substantial emendation seems to be necessary.
3.33-3.34 tamolaḥ I pana[1+]taranya • Cf. Cane tamolah ṅke ri sīmanya I cane kapaṅgiha deni savkavetnya. See also the passages cited two notes below.
4.5 sara¿ny?⟨sanya⟩ • See Baru, faces Cd lines 8 and 39, and Gandhakuti plate IV, verso, line 5.
4.25 ta(n· to)liha • The presence of a large irregularity in the surface of the stone and the descending parts of the word sŭkṣma in line 24 make it hard to recognize the expected segment n· to.
9.1 dhunenavana givihikən·, cagəm·, ṅayai • Seemingly only proper names, none recognizable with certainty.
