1r1-1r3Homage be to the six Vināyakas! Hail! Elapsed Śaka year 861, month of Bhadrapada, fifth tithi of the waning fortnight, Vās, Pahiṅ, Friday , lunar mansion Aśvinī, deity Aśvī, conjunction Viṣkambha. That was the time that the decree of the Great King, rakai Halu, dyah Siṇḍok Śrī Īśānavikrama, received by the rakryān minister of Halu, dyah Sahasra, descended to the officiant of Kanuruhan, pu Udā, instructing with regard to the varuk land at Ālasantan, district of Bavaṅ Mapapan, that it be made the freehold of (or: that it be made into a freehold by) the rakryān kabayān, mother of rakryān minister of Halu, dyah Sahasra.

1r8-1r14The purpose of the land being made a freehold was to become a paṅurumbigyan freehold of the rakryān kabayān. Its status would be independent. It should not be entered as before by the patihs, the vahutas, the headmen, and all types of collectors of royal revenues: various miśras, various types of vulu, paṅuraṅ, kriṅ, paḍam, manimpiki, paraṇakan, limus galuḥ, paṅaruhan, taji, vatu tajam, sukun, halu varak, rakadut, pinilai, kataṅgaran, tapa haji, air haji, malandaṅ, ləca, ləblab, kalaṅkaṅ, kutak, taṅkil, tr̥pan, salvīt, vatu palaṅ, pamaṇikan, sikpān, rumbān, tiṅkəs, viji kavaḥ, vilaṅ vanua, tuha dagaṅ, juru guśali, tuhān nambi, tuhān huñjaman, juru juḍi, juru jalir, miśra hino, vli hapū, vli vaduṅ, vli tambaṅ, vli pañjut, vli harəṅ, pabisar, palamak, pakaluṅkuṅ, urutan, dampulan, tpuṅ kavuṅ, suṅsuṅ paṅuraṅ, forest dwellers (pasuk alas), payuṅan, pāṅin-aṅin, sipad bilut, puluṅ paḍi, skar tahun, panrāṅan, panusuh, hopan, mahouts (pahaliman), eunuchs (kḍi) healesr (valyan), actors (vidu), sambal, sumbul, royal servants (hulun haji), pamr̥ṣi, sinaguha, the courtiers (vatak i jro), etc., shall not enter into the paṅurumbigyan freehold of the varuk land at Ālasantan. Only the rakryān founder of the freehold herself shall have authority over all its royal revenues.

2r1-2r7Likewise the numbers of those who engage in trade while present there are restricted, [that is] those not subjected to the enjoyers of royal dues is four tradesman of the same trade per freehold. If they buy buffaloes, the share of buffaloes is 20 per tradesman; 40 cattle per tradesman; 80 sheep per tradesman; one flock/shelter of ducks per tradesman; silver-smiths, four masters; those who drive ox-carts, four yokes; drums, three places to play (make?) them; carpenters, two tradesman; goldsmiths, two smithies; coppersmiths, two smithies; ironsmiths, two smithies; cadar-clothmakers, eight cadar-clothmaking places. If their wares are carried with carrying-poles — such as cloth vendors, tinkers (vendors of copper utensils), condiment sellers, fence makers, transporters of cotton, mengkudu, iron, copper, brass, tin, salt blocks, sesame, uncooked rice, sugar, safflower (seeds?) — all types of wares that are carred with poles: altogether there will be five carrying-pole loads per tradesman. Those who are in conformity with that will not be subjected to the enjoyers of royal dues, wherever [those traders] might go, whatever may be there origin. However, they should keep a document to that effect. If [their trade] is more than the limit [imposed] on them, they will be subjected in accordance with the surplus to the enjoyers of one royal share sa-uddhāra. It will not be exceeded.

3r14-3r17 After giving the gifts with cloth to all of them, the offerings for the Makudur were placed below the canopy. The one serving as priest poured water to purify the ‘heart’ for piercing and the kulumpaṅ stone. The Makudur arises bringing sembah to the holy ‘heart’ and sits below the canopy, having his feet as support (i.e. knelt), facing the holy ‘heart’, wearing as siṅhəl 1 set of cloth. The Vadihati follows. The Makudur begins to uyut, and cleaves the neck of a chicken, using the kulumpaṅ-stone as underlay, and bashes an egg on the sīma stone, prononouncing admonition and imprecation adressed to all those who are customarily admonished so that the holy freehold will be respected:

3r17-XX‘Pay heed you gods at Śrī Haricandana, Agastya Mahārṣi, - East, South, West, North, Center, Zenith, Nadir; - Sun, Moon, Earth, Water, Wind, Fire, Yajamāna, Ether; - Dharma, Day and Night, Twilight, Heart; - Yakṣas, Rākṣasas, Piśācas, Pretas, Asuras, Garuḍa, Gandharvas; - Four Lokapālas: Yama, Varuṇa, Kubera, Vāsava (Indra ) - And sons of the gods: Five Kuśikas, Nandīśvara, Mahākāla, Six Vināyakas, Snake-Kings, goddess Durgā - Quadrangular ones (Aśvins?), Ananta, Surendra; - children of Kāla and Mṛtyu: Gaṇas and Bhūtas - you well-known ones who protect the palace of his majesty the king at Mḍaṁ in the land of Matarām - you who take part in embodying, entering in all bodies - you all who as witness watch far and near, at day and by night, as you listen to the agreement, the curse, the oath, the admission of us to all you gods: - if the bad man does not hold firm, does not safeguard that curse being deposited by the Vahuta Hyaṅ, the Kudur, Lord and servant, old and young, man and female, religious man and householder, as well as minister, Vahuta and headman, - whoever disturbs the varuk land at Ālasantan as Paṅurumbigyan freehold, bestowed by his majesty the king on the venerable Kabayān, mother of the lord minister of Halu [called] Dyah Sahasra, up to the future’s future may split asunder, all the more so if he shall uproot the holy sīma stone. Therefore, even if he would be killed by you, o gods, your approach when you kill him hould be without looking back, not looking to the side, clash with the opponent, slap his left side, attack anew his right side, cut down his snout, split his skull, rip open his belly, stretch out his intestines, draw out his entrails, tear out his liver, without delay finish off, bring to an end his life. If he goes to the forest, may he be eaten up by the tiger, bitten by the snake, whirled round and round by divine wrath! If he goes to the field, may he be caught by thunderclap, may his head be torn off by demons, may he be eaten by Pamuṅvan giants! Unceasingly in his home all diseases torment him! Please you gods Kuśika, Garga, Metrī, Kuruṣya and Pātañjala, guardian of the North, guardian of the South, of the West, of the East, throw [him] in the air, tear [him] asunder (?) among all gods, throw [him] into the great ocean, drown [him] at the dam! May he be caught by the holy gods in the water! May he be pulled by sea-monsters! May he be snatched by the crocodile! May it be there that he dies, the lawless man who destroys the Paṅurumbigyan-freehold at Ālasantan. May he suffer misfortune among all devatas (deceased ancestors)! May he go to ruins, may be be engulfed in the Phīra (or Dhīra: ocean)! May he end up plunged into hell! May he be dropped in the Great Raurava! May he be cooked by the forces of Yama! May he be hammered seven times by the Kiṅkaras (servant of Yama). Surely, when he is given shape, it will be a bad one. Surely he will suffer as long as he lives! May all types of pain be experienced by him! May all sorts of pain be undergone by him! Every detestable thing of rebirth is what he will become! May he smoulder without being extinguised! As fanned ash, thus will become the lawless man who destroys the Paṅurumbigyan-freehold at Ālasantan!

Commentary

1r3The term varuk is not well understood. Zoetmulder 1982: s.v. varuk glosses ‘a piece of ground (acquired in a part. way? bought?)’, while Wibowo 1979: 13 tentatively suggested: ‘Mungkin kata lmaḥ waruk menunjuk bagian yang masih hutan belukar’.

1r5-7The headword of each entry in the list (vinkas, apkan, gusti, etc.) is directly followed by a name, further names separated by median dots.

1r8The word paṅurumbigyan is derived, with pa-...-an circumfix, from kurumbigi which seems to be an alternate of kurumbhāgi, ‘a kind of iron tool (knife)’ (Zoetmulder 1982: s.v. ). The compound term sīma paṅurumbigyan is reminiscent of the much more common pattern sīma ka-...-an (sīma kajurugusalyan, sīma kavajvan, sīma kakaṭikan, etc.) seen in numerous Old Javanese inscriptions.

1r14-16I adopt the interpretation of the term sukha-duḥkha itself, and of the meanings of the items in the list, from work in progress by Timothy Lubin.
